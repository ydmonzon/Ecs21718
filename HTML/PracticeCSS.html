Rosa Parks

The First Lady of Civil Rights

Rosa Parks rode at the front of a Montgomery, Alabama,bus on the day the Supreme Court's ban on segregation of the city's buses took effect. She was arrested for violating a city law requiring racial segregation of public buses. Sge refused to give her seat up to a white man who wanted to sit where she was.

Achievements

  • On September 9, 1996, President Bill Clinton presented Rosa Parks the Presidential Medal of Freedom Award.
  • In 1979 she was awarded the Spingarn Medal by the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People for outstanding achievement even while being an African American.
  • In 1995 the Golden Plate Award was presented to her
  • In 1999 she was awarded the Congressional Gold Medal by the United States Congress
  • In 2000 she was awarded the NAACP Image Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Drama Series in the film Touched By An Angel
